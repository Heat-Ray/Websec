       <p>
       In addition to helping game developers with my articles, I love
       to talk to people working with games and interactive
       education. Mostly it's informal chats over lunch, but
       occasionally I'll work with someone for a longer period of
       time.

       <li><a href="http://www.root-1.com/">Root-1</a> was building
       educational games for learning language, starting with

       adapt to each student's needs. They're in Fast Company's list of
       <a
       href="http://www.fastcompany.com/most-innovative-companies/2012/industry/education#root1">the
       world's 50 most innovative companies</a>, and tested their games

       Word Kung Fu, and XWords are available <a

     --><section><h2><span>About me</span></h2><p>I’ve been helping people make games since 1990. I wrote games earlier in life, with <a href="http://breakintochat.com/wiki/Solar_Realms_Elite">Solar Realms Elite</a><sup class="print-endnote">[22]</sup> being the <a href="http://breakintochat.com/blog/2013/02/13/solar-realms-elite-memories/">most well known</a><sup class="print-endnote">[23]</sup>, then worked on an environmental simulation game called <a href="/blobcity/">BlobCity</a>, then took a break for over a decade. The recent rise of indie, mobile, tablet, social, and web games have made me interested in game development again. My current passion is using interactivity on the web for learning, especially computer science and math. I’ve started with game development because it’s a rich source of interesting problems that can be explained visually. With modern web browsers, we can use explanations that don’t follow the formats used in magazines, technical papers, and books. We can combine learning by reading, learning by watching, and <a href="http://explorabl.es/">learning by doing</a><sup class="print-endnote">[24]</sup>.</p><p>I previously worked with <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/most-innovative-companies/2012/industry/education#root1">Root-1</a><sup class="print-endnote">[25]</sup> on educational games, Wild Shadow Studios on <a href="http://www.metacritic.com/game/pc/realm-of-the-mad-god">Realm of the Mad God</a><sup class="print-endnote">[26]</sup>, Peter Norvig on interactive diagrams for <a href="http://aima.cs.berkeley.edu/">Artifical Intelligence: A Modern Approach</a><sup class="print-endnote">[27]</sup>, and Nonagon Games on <a href="https://www.achronicleofmisdeeds.com/">A Chronicle of Misdeeds</a><sup class="print-endnote">[28]</sup>.</p><p>In addition to helping game developers with my articles, I love to talk to people working with games and interactive education. Mostly it’s informal chats over lunch, but occasionally I’ll work with someone for a longer period of time. If you’re developing games in the Palo Alto / San Jose area and want to chat in person, email me at <a href="mailto:redblobgames@gmail.com">redblobgames@gmail.com</a>. I’m especially interested in game algorithms related to maps, procedural generation, and pathfinding, but love to chat about anything.</p><!--
